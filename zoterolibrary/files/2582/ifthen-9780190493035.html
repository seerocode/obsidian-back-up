Algorithmic Power and Politics

Taina Bucher

Oxford Studies in Digital Politics

  • The first book-length work to critically discuss the socio-technical aspects of algorithmic power
  • Argues for a renewed software criticism opposed to using software merely as a tool to analyze social data
  • Offers a theoretical framework for the kinds of work that algorithms do and the new media environment they help to shape
